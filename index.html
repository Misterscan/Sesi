Core Primitives

⎈

Distributed Orchestration

Orchestrate complex systems with ease. Sesi uses the native spawn() primitive to launch and coordinate concurrent processes that communicate through atomic state management.

⚙

Native System Primitives

Native access to time(), random(), and exec() builtins. Sesi provides a robust abstraction layer for systems-level control within a high-performance interpreter environment.

🛡

Resilient Orchestration

Native try/catch logic handles the unpredictability of distributed systems. Sesi ensures your orchestration remains robust through filesystem contention and network shifts.

🖧

Filesystem-as-a-Bus

Eliminate complex message brokers. Sesi treats the filesystem as a high-speed, persistent communication bus for effortless state synchronization between processes.

🧠

Integrated Reasoning

Reasoning is a first-class language primitive. Use model() and structured_output() to integrate advanced logic directly into your systems orchestration without external SDKs.

⚡

Atomic Coordination

Achieve atomic precision without the boilerplate. Sesi's systems primitives allow for distributed coordination that is as reliable as it is simple to write.

Simplicity as a Primitive

Traditional systems engineering is buried in boilerplate. Sesi eliminates the noise by making high-level intent a native language construct. Why write 100 lines of SDK glue code when you can express the same logic in 4 lines of Sesi?

By integrating Reasoning Primitives directly into the runtime, Sesi understands the context of your filesystem, your processes, and your goals. It doesn't just execute code; it orchestrates systems with the awareness of a senior engineer.

From distributed state management to complex data synthesis, Sesi proves that the most powerful systems are the ones that are the easiest to read.
